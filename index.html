A Guide for Students

Have you ever wondered why you act the way you do? The answer may lie in which side of your brain you tend to use more. Popular theories suggest that right-brain people have different skills and preferences than those who are left-brained. How do these do these differences impact you? Read on to find out!

The left & right hemispheres both manage different functions, each attributed to different characteristics and abilities.

Hover over a hemisphere to see what it controls

Traits of left-brained people

  • Things are done in a planned orderly way

  • Decisions are made with reason and logic

  • Skilled at math

  • Things are done accurately

  • Has practical expectations of outcomes

  • Prefers classical music

Traits of right-brained people

  • Things are done spontaneously

  • Tends to act on feelings

  • Full of imagination

  • Doesn't like instructions

  • Interested in the unexplained

  • Prefers rock music

Advice for left-brained students

  • Volunteer to lead groups and projects

  • Plan ahead if feeling overwhelmed

  • Try not to argue with the teacher

  • Choose factual over open assignments

  • Work alone when you have a choice

  • Avoid free-thinking teachers

Advice for right-brained students

  • Prioritize finishing what you start

  • Keep interest by using colourful language in essays

  • Stay on track by writing down a plan

  • Avoid thinking of all the possibilities when answering questions

  • Take time to organize plans and thoughts

  • Avoid teachers who lecture a lot
